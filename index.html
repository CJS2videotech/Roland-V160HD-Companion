Live Production Pro Tips

Power Sequencing Rules

Always power on the network switcher first, followed by your cameras, and finally the V-160HD. When powering down, reverse the order to prevent HDMI handshake errors.

Gain-Staging Master Rule

Keep physical faders close to Unity (0dB). If the signal is too quiet or clipping, adjust the digital pre-amp Gain or Attenuation in the menus, not the fader.

EDID Emulator Locks

Lock the Input EDID in the switcher menu (VIDEO INPUT -> EDID) to 1080p for client laptops. This prevents the laptop from changing resolutions when screens sleep.

Macros vs. Presets

Presets (Scenes) WHAT THEY ARE:

Instant snapshots capturing the static state of the entire switcher (active inputs, PinP layout sizes, keyers, and fader levels).

WHEN TO USE THEM:

Use presets to transition cleanly between distinct show segments (e.g., switching from "Solo Presenter" layout to a "Two-Shot Panel" layout).

Macros (Automation) WHAT THEY ARE:

Recorded script sequences that execute a series of manual button pushes and actions over a timeline.

WHEN TO USE THEM:

Use macros to automate rapid multi-button operations that are too fast or complex for manual switching (e.g., fade keyer, wait 1s, cut inputs, slide graphic).

The Key Difference

A Preset is a static "destination state" (where everything is). A Macro is a dynamic "flight path" (the sequential actions taken to get somewhere).

Broadcast Glossary

PGM (Program)

The active, live output signal being broadcasted or recorded. What the audience sees.

PST (Preset)

The preview bus showing the next video source cued up and ready to transition.

AUX (Auxiliary)

A separate video or audio bus used to route signals independently of the main PGM output.

EDID

Extended Display Identification Data. Metadata sent by a display to source devices describing its resolution capabilities.

HDCP

High-bandwidth Digital Content Protection. A form of digital copy protection that prevents copying of content across HDMI.

Unity Gain

A setting on an audio level control where output level equals input level (typically marked as 0dB or U).
